# Amp Energy — Blog Brand Voice Guide

## Voice & Tone
- **Authoritative but accessible**: Write like a senior industry expert explaining things to a smart peer, not a professor lecturing students.
- **Data-driven**: Always ground insights in numbers and specifics. Amp's key stats: 10–20% energy savings identified, <1 year payback, 24-hour deployment, 0.2% margin of error, 64% background load reduction (case study).
- **Practical and action-oriented**: Every article should leave the reader with something concrete they can do or decide. Avoid purely theoretical content.
- **Regionally aware**: Primary markets are UAE and KSA. Reference DEWA, UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 11, Vision 2050, Vision 2030, GCC building codes, local climate realities (extreme cooling loads).
- **Confident, not salesy**: Amp is referenced naturally as a solution where relevant, but never in a hard-sell tone. The blog educates first. The CTA is always soft — "Book a Demo" or "Contact Us" at the end.

## What We Never Do
- Use empty buzzwords without backing them up ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge")
- Make claims without data
- Write generic content that could apply to any energy company anywhere
- Ignore the regional context (this isn't a US or EU audience)
- Write overly technical content that alienates non-engineers (our primary reader is a facilities manager, not a data scientist)

## Content Structure
Every blog post follows this structure:
1. **Hook** (2–3 sentences): Lead with a pain point, stat, or timely event
2. **Context / Problem** (150–250 words): Frame the industry challenge
3. **Insight / Solution** (300–500 words): Educational content, practical guidance
4. **Amp Connection** (50–100 words): Natural, non-salesy reference to Amp's relevance
5. **Conclusion + CTA** (50–100 words): Summary takeaway + soft CTA
6. **FAQ Section** (2–3 questions): Structured for AI Overview / featured snippet eligibility

## SEO Requirements
- Title: Contains primary keyword, under 60 characters, compelling
- Meta description: 150–160 characters, includes keyword, drives clicks
- H2/H3 hierarchy: Logical, keyword-aware headings
- Internal links: Reference 1–2 other Amp blog posts or pages (product, contact) where natural
- FAQ: Always include 2–3 structured FAQ items at the end

## Target Article Length
800–1,200 words (excluding FAQ section)

## Personas We Write For
1. **The Facility Champion**: FM managers who know energy is being wasted but can't prove it. Practical, evidence-based, risk-averse.
2. **The Sustainability Strategist**: ESG/sustainability leaders who need auditable Scope 2 data. Strategic, compliance-oriented.
3. **The Executive Sponsor**: C-suite who approve budgets. Care about ROI, risk, and competitive positioning.
4. **The Procurement Gatekeeper**: Process-focused. Needs vendor credibility, references, specs.
5. **The Channel Multiplier**: ESCOs, solar companies, FM companies who want to differentiate their offering.

## Key Product Differentiators to Weave In Naturally
- No BMS required — works on any building
- Equipment-level granularity (not just building-level)
- 24-hour deployment, zero downtime
- Lowest cost per endpoint in market
- Clamp-on sensors, no electrical work
- AI-powered savings recommendations
- Portfolio-wide multi-site visibility
- Direct relevance to UAE Decree-Law No. 11 Scope 2 compliance
